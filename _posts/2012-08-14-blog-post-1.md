The Quantum Fourier Transform (QFT) is a fundamental operation in quantum computing that generalizes the classical discrete Fourier transform to quantum states. It plays a crucial role in many quantum algorithms, including Shor's algorithm for factoring.

## Mathematical Foundation

The QFT transforms a quantum state $|\psi\rangle = \sum_{j=0}^{N-1} x_j |j\rangle$ into:

$$|\psi'\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \sum_{k=0}^{N-1} \left(\sum_{j=0}^{N-1} x_j e^{2\pi i jk/N}\right) |k\rangle$$

where $N = 2^n$ for an $n$-qubit system.

## Circuit Implementation

The QFT can be implemented using a series of Hadamard gates and controlled phase rotations. For a 3-qubit system, the circuit looks like:

```
|q₀⟩ ──H──R₂──R₃──
|q₁⟩ ─────H──R₂──
|q₂⟩ ────────H──
```

where $R_k$ represents a rotation by $2\pi/2^k$.

## Applications

The QFT is essential in:
- **Shor's Algorithm**: For finding the period of a function
- **Quantum Phase Estimation**: For estimating eigenvalues
- **Quantum Machine Learning**: In quantum feature maps
