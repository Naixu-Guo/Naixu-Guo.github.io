The Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) is a hybrid quantum-classical algorithm for finding the ground state energy of a given Hamiltonian. It combines the power of quantum computers with classical optimization techniques.

## Problem Formulation

Given a Hamiltonian $H$, we want to find the ground state $|\psi_0\rangle$ that minimizes:

$$\langle\psi|H|\psi\rangle$$

subject to the constraint $\langle\psi|\psi\rangle = 1$.

## Algorithm Structure

1. **Parameterized Quantum Circuit**: We use a parameterized quantum state $|\psi(\theta)\rangle = U(\theta)|0\rangle$

2. **Expectation Estimation**: Measure $\langle\psi(\theta)|H|\psi(\theta)\rangle$ on the quantum computer

3. **Classical Optimization**: Use classical optimizers to find $\theta^* = \arg\min_\theta \langle\psi(\theta)|H|\psi(\theta)\rangle$

## Mathematical Details

The cost function can be written as:

$$C(\theta) = \sum_i c_i \langle\psi(\theta)|P_i|\psi(\theta)\rangle$$

where $H = \sum_i c_i P_i$ is the Pauli decomposition of the Hamiltonian.

## Advantages and Limitations

**Advantages:**
- Works on noisy quantum computers
- Requires fewer qubits than exact diagonalization
- Can handle large molecular systems

**Limitations:**
- Convergence depends on ansatz choice
- Classical optimization can be challenging
- Limited by quantum noise

This algorithm represents a promising approach for near-term quantum applications in chemistry and materials science.
